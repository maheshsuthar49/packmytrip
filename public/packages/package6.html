Chardham Yatra

The Char Dham Yatra in Uttarakhand—Badrinath, Kedarnath, Gangotri, and Yamunotri—is a revered Hindu pilgrimage, believed to cleanse the soul and lead to salvation (moksha). Situated in the Garhwal Himalayas, these shrines attract devotees from across India and beyond. Badrinath is dedicated to Lord Vishnu, Kedarnath to Lord Shiva, Gangotri to Goddess Ganga, and Yamunotri to Goddess Yamuna. These sacred sites hold immense significance in Hindu mythology. Additionally, another Char Dham, established by Adi Shankaracharya, consists of Badrinath (Uttarakhand), Dwarka (Gujarat), Puri (Odisha), and Rameswaram (Tamil Nadu), forming four major spiritual destinations across India.

Itinerary

Day1

Arrival in Haridwar - Rishikesh

Your journey begins in Haridwar, one of India's holiest cities. Visit Har Ki Pauri to witness the mesmerizing Ganga Aarti in the evening. Later, proceed to Rishikesh and explore Ram Jhula, Laxman Jhula, and Triveni Ghat before an overnight stay.

Day2

Rishikesh - Barkot

After breakfast, leave for Barkot, passing through the scenic town of Mussoorie. Stop at Kempty Falls to enjoy the breathtaking views. Continue your journey through lush valleys and check into your hotel in Barkot for an overnight stay.

Day3

Barkot - Yamunotri - Barkot

Early morning, drive to Janki Chatti and trek 6 km to Yamunotri Temple. Take a dip in Surya Kund before offering prayers to Goddess Yamuna. After darshan, trek back to Janki Chatti and return to Barkot.

Day4

Barkot - Uttarkashi

After breakfast, drive to Uttarkashi, a town on the banks of the Bhagirathi River. Visit the famous Kashi Vishwanath Temple, dedicated to Lord Shiva. Spend the evening at leisure before an overnight stay.

Day5

Uttarkashi - Gangotri - Uttarkashi

Drive through scenic valleys to Gangotri. Stop at Gangnani for a dip in the Rishikund hot spring. At Gangotri Temple, seek blessings from Goddess Ganga and visit Surya Kund before returning to Uttarkashi for the night.

Day6

Uttarkashi - Guptkashi

Leave early for Guptkashi, passing through mesmerizing landscapes and the Mandakini River. Upon arrival, visit the Vishwanath Temple and check into your hotel for an overnight stay.

Day7

Guptkashi - Kedarnath

Drive to Gaurikund, the base for the 18 km trek to Kedarnath. Trek or opt for pony/doli/helicopter services. Upon arrival, visit Kedarnath Temple and offer prayers to Lord Shiva. Stay overnight in Kedarnath.

Day8

Kedarnath - Guptkashi

Attend the morning Aarti at Kedarnath Temple. Trek back to Gaurikund and drive to Guptkashi for a well-deserved rest.

Day9

Guptkashi - Badrinath

Proceed towards Badrinath, stopping at Joshimath. Upon arrival, take a dip in Tapt Kund before visiting Badrinath Temple. Explore Mana Village, Vyas Gufa, and Vasudhara Falls before an overnight stay.

Day10

Badrinath - Rudraprayag

Attend the morning Aarti at Badrinath and visit Brahma Kapal for ancestor rituals. Drive towards Rudraprayag, where the Alaknanda and Mandakini Rivers merge. Stay overnight in Rudraprayag.

Day11

Rudraprayag - Haridwar

Drive back to Haridwar, stopping at Devprayag, where the Bhagirathi and Alaknanda Rivers form the Ganga. Attend the Ganga Aarti at Har Ki Pauri before resting for the night.

Day12

Departure from Haridwar

After breakfast, check out and proceed to Delhi or your onward destination, marking the end of your spiritually fulfilling Char Dham Yatra.
